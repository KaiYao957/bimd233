Circles make the world go round, because they are one of the most important shapes in the universe. (Pun intended, because circles are literally round. The most important thing about a circle is that it's round.) There is a unique number associated with the roundness of the circle, known as, you guessed it, pi. Pi, the ratio of the circumference (the length around the circle) to the diameter (the length across the circle), is one of the most important irrational numbers, with a value of approximately 3.14159. Highly advanced computers have computed pi to as many digits as possible to test their amazing power. There's a reason why pi rhymes with pie! Well, this "reason" I mentioned is in reality just a coincidence. That still doesn't stop us from making jokes about pi like the "pi rhyming with pie" joke. Did you know that Albert Einstein was born on Pi Day? (Which is by the way, on March 14) It's very fitting that one of the smartest scientists who ever lived, the guy who discovered the theory of relativity, E=mc2 and some of the most important physics discoveries ever made, was born on the day that looks similar to one of the most important numbers known to humanity.
